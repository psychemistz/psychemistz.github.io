I am a postdoctoral research fellow at the Cancer Data Science Laboratory (CDSL), NCI/CCR, NIH. I develop computational methods to infer cell-to-cell communication signals from gene expression data. My research spans a fourteen-year arc from experimental microfluidic systems for synthetic bacterial signaling (2011--2012) to large-scale computational inference of cytokine and secreted-protein activity from bulk, single-cell, and spatial transcriptomics.

Current contributions include IC2Bert for immune-checkpoint-blockade response prediction (\textit{Scientific Reports}, 2025), a co-authored analysis of AI in precision oncology (\textit{Nature Cancer}, 2025), and the open-source activity-inference tools \textbf{SecActPy} (Python), \textbf{spatial-gpu} (GPU-accelerated spatial kernels), and \textbf{RidgeR} (R). Further work at NCI/CDSL is ongoing.

My methodological interests include prior-informed machine learning for clinical biomarker discovery (disease gene prioritization, ensemble subspace learning), sparse representation learning for biomolecular classification, overlap-based effect-size measures (GSSMD, GMDM) for data and model quality evaluation, and self-supervised learning for biosignal reproducibility (SERS, phonocardiograms). I have authored or co-authored peer-reviewed work across \textit{Nature Cancer}, \textit{Scientific Reports}, \textit{Analyst}, \textit{Biomaterials}, \textit{Lab on a Chip}, \textit{Cancers}, and \textit{Digital Signal Processing}, along with three issued patents.

\end{cvparagraph}
